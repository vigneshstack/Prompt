First, what is Intelligence?

The ability to perceive or infer information, and to retain it as knowledge to be applied towards adaptive behaviors within an environment or context.

In simple terms: Learning, reasoning, and problem-solving.

And what is Artificial Intelligence?

It's when we build machines that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence.

It's not about consciousness or feeling; it's about simulating intelligent behavior.

The Dream of Thinking Machines

For centuries, humans have imagined creating artificial beings that can think.

In 1950, computer science pioneer Alan Turing proposed a test for machine intelligence, asking:

"Can machines think?"

This shifted the question from a philosophical debate to a tangible engineering problem.

The Birth of a Name

The term "Artificial Intelligence" was coined in 1956.

It happened at the Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence, a workshop that brought together the founding fathers of the field.

They laid the groundwork for decades of research to come.

From Words to Numbers: Tokenization

AI models don't understand words. They understand numbers (tokens).

Your input is broken down into a sequence of tokens.

"AI is powerful"

"AI"
"is"
"power"
"ful"

Each token corresponds to a number the model can process.

Using AI Tools: Do's & Don'ts

Do ✅

  • Verify important information
  • Use it to brainstorm & draft
  • Be clear and specific in prompts
  • Review and edit the output

Don't ❌

  • Trust it blindly ("hallucinations")
  • Submit AI work as your own
  • Share sensitive or private data
  • Use it for high-stakes decisions

Ethical & Responsible Use

As we build and use AI, we must consider:

  • Bias: AI models can learn and amplify human biases from their training data.
  • Privacy: How is our data being used to train and operate these models?
  • Accountability: Who is responsible when an AI makes a mistake?
  • Transparency: Can we understand how the AI reached its conclusion?
